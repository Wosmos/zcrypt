The boundary

There's a line your files cross.
We're on the wrong side of it.

Everything sensitive happens on your device. What crosses to our servers is already unreadable — no keys, no names, no plaintext.

No recovery — on purpose

If you lose your passphrase, your files are gone.

We know how that sounds. But it's the proof the rest is true: a service that could recover your files isn't one that can't read them. Use a password manager — we'd rather be honest than offer a back door.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Can zcrypt see my files?+

No. Files are encrypted on your device with AES-256-GCM before upload, using a key derived from your passphrase — which never leaves your machine. Our servers only ever hold encrypted chunks.

What does "bring your own storage" mean?+

You connect your own GitHub, GitLab, or Hugging Face account. zcrypt encrypts and chunks your files, then stores the encrypted blobs in repositories you own and control. Your data, your infrastructure.

Is it really open source?+

Yes — the whole thing. Don't take our word for the crypto; read it. Links to the exact modules are in the section above.

Is there a terminal app?+

Yes. A full Go TUI with vim-style navigation and the same zero-knowledge encryption. A single binary, no dependencies.

What if I forget my passphrase?+

It can't be recovered — by design. There's nothing on our side to recover it from. Store it in a password manager.

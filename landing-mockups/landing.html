Lifecycle

The full journey of a file.

From your device and back — every step is yours to verify.

STEP 01

You drop a file

Select anything on your device. Nothing has left it yet — and nothing will, in the clear.

STEP 02 · zstd

Compressed

The file is squeezed down so it travels smaller and stores leaner.

STEP 03 · AES-256-GCM

Encrypted on-device

Locked with a key derived from your passphrase. The key never leaves your machine.

STEP 04

Split into chunks

Broken into ~10 MB encrypted pieces — enabling unlimited file sizes across platform limits.

STEP 05

Distributed to your clouds

Chunks land as ordinary-looking files in your own GitHub, GitLab, Hugging Face, or Telegram.

ANYTIME

Retrieved & rebuilt

Download reverses it all: chunks reassemble, decrypt, and decompress — only with your passphrase.

Why zcrypt

Built so you never have to trust us.

Every layer engineered for privacy — and every line of it open source.

01 / ENCRYPTION

Encrypted before it ever leaves your device

Keys derive from your passphrase client-side and are never transmitted. AES-256-GCM detects tampering, not just blocks it. There's no key on our side — we literally cannot decrypt your data.

$ zcrypt push research.tar
# compress → encrypt → chunk
✓ sealed 3 chunks
✓ key never left device
02 / SCALE

Effectively unlimited storage

Files split into ~10 MB chunks and upload in parallel. zcrypt watches repo sizes and auto-provisions a fresh repository before any platform limit — you're bounded only by the free space you already own.

03 / STEALTH

Nothing flags your vault

Chunks commit with plausible filenames, randomized messages, and ordinary repository names. Your storage repos blend in as unremarkable projects — by design.

04 / OPEN

Open source & self-hostable

Audit the encryption yourself, or run the entire backend on your own infrastructure. Bring-your-own-backend is available to everyone. Your trust is never assumed — it's earned by code you can read.

Zero knowledge

Plaintext never crosses the wire.

The encryption boundary lives entirely on your device. By the time anything is transmitted, it's already ciphertext we have no way to open.

Keys derived locally

Your passphrase generates the key on-device. Never sent, stored, or recoverable by us.

Only ciphertext is uploaded

Storage backends — even your own — only ever see opaque encrypted chunks.

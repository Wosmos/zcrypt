The boundary

What leaves your machine is already unreadable.

zcrypt is precise about where encryption happens. The passphrase is used locally to derive a key with PBKDF2-SHA256 at 600,000 iterations. Each file gets a random content encryption key, and that key is wrapped before anything crosses the network.

Your devicecan see plaintext
  • zstd compression

    Files are compressed before encryption, once, in the upload pipeline.

  • AES-256-GCM encryption

    A random CEK encrypts each file. Your passphrase-derived key wraps the CEK.

  • Sharing via URL fragments

    Share keys travel after the # in the URL, so browsers do not send them to the server.

Server and storageciphertext only
  • Encrypted chunks

    Chunked blobs, about 10 MB each, live on the storage backend you connect.

  • Encrypted index and metadata

    zcrypt keeps an encrypted index/metadata database, not readable file contents.

  • No passphrase recovery

    If you lose the passphrase, the files are unrecoverable by design.

BYOB storage

Bring the backend. Keep the control.

zcrypt does not ask you to trust a new storage silo. You connect accounts you already control, and zcrypt writes encrypted, chunked blobs there. The storage provider sees bytes, not files.

Open source now. Storage is yours.

The product is free and open-source. The tradeoff is honest: capacity and platform limits come from your connected backend account, not a storage plan sold by zcrypt.

FAQ

Short answers for skeptical people.

The security story gets stronger when the uncomfortable details are easy to find.

Can zcrypt read my files?+

No. Files are encrypted on your device before upload. zcrypt's server can handle encrypted chunks and encrypted index/metadata, but it never receives plaintext or your passphrase-derived keys.

How does sharing stay zero-knowledge?+

Each file has a content-encryption key. For sharing, that CEK is wrapped with a per-share key, and the share key travels only in the URL fragment, the part after #. Browsers do not send fragments to servers.

What happens if I lose my passphrase?+

Your files are unrecoverable. That is deliberate: zcrypt never receives the passphrase, so there is nothing for the service to reset or recover.

What does BYOB mean in practice?+

You connect your own GitHub, GitLab, or Hugging Face account. zcrypt writes encrypted, chunked blobs there. Your total capacity is bounded by the backend account you bring.

Is the desktop app production-polished?+

Not yet. zcrypt is in active development. The web app and Go TUI are the primary product surfaces right now, and the desktop app is still maturing.

Why open source?+

Because "trust us" is the wrong answer for encrypted storage. The strongest version of zcrypt is one you can verify by reading the code.
